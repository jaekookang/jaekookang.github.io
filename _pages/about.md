[ghang, dʒe.gu]  


Speech is critical for our daily lives. We deliver linguistic messages, share feelings and even build our identities to name a few. We can't see it, but we know and understand speech. How is it possible? Specifically, how is the system of producing speech connected to the perception of speech? What are the factors affecthing this relationship? These are my core questions during my Ph.D. journey in Speech-Language-Hearing Sciences program at CUNY Graduate Center, NY, USA.


Research Interests:
- Variability in speech production and perception
- Computational modeling of speech including:
	- Speech representations
	- Speech recognition
	- Speech synthesis
	- Voice conversion
